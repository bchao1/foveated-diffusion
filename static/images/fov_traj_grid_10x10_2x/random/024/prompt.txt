A single slice of Neapolitan margherita pizza sits on a wooden cutting board, the thin charred crust, the bright red San Marzano tomato sauce, the pools of melted buffalo mozzarella, and the fresh basil leaves vivid and sharp. A drizzle of green olive oil glistens on the cheese's surface, and a scatter of flour dust and a few charred bubbles mark the crust's underside where it meets the board. The cutting board's knife scars, the marble counter beneath it, the flour-dusted pizza peel leaning against the wall behind, and the wood-fired oven's dark mouth at the far edge are all uniformly in focus. The light is the warm glow of the oven mixed with flat kitchen overhead.

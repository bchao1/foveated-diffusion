The Banaue rice terraces descend in two-thousand-year-old steps from a cloud-forest ridge to the river valley below in the Philippine Cordillera, each terrace wall a precise curve of hand-stacked stone holding a shallow pool of water that mirrors the sky. The green rice shoots, the stone retaining walls, and the moss-covered irrigation channels are all uniformly sharp from the nearest paddy to the distant ridge. The valley holds a thin layer of morning mist, the surrounding peaks are dark with forest, and the overcast light renders every terrace step with equal clarity.

A turquoise hot spring steams in a Yellowstone geyser basin, the mineral-stained rings of orange, white, and emerald terracing outward from the boiling centre to the cooler edges. The surrounding basin is a vast plain of white silica flats stretching to distant lodgepole pine forest under a cold blue sky, and the steam column rises straight and thick from the spring's eye. Every mineral deposit, every thermophile colour band, and every crack in the sinter crust is uniformly sharp, and the low winter sun paints the steam in gold against the dark treeline.

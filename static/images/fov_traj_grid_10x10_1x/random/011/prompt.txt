The Matterhorn rises as a near-perfect pyramid of dark rock and snow above an alpine meadow, the east face in full morning sun, the north face in blue shadow. The ridgelines—the Hörnli, the Zmuttgrat, the Furgggrat, and the Italian ridge—are all sharp and defined, and the glacier at the base is a cracked field of blue-white ice. The alpine meadow in the foreground holds the last wildflowers of summer, and every element from the nearest gentian to the summit rocks is in equal, unforgiving focus against a sky of deep cobalt.

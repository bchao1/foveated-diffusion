Zhangjiajie's sandstone pillar forest rises from a sea of green canopy in Hunan province, hundreds of narrow quartzite columns standing hundreds of metres tall, their flat tops crowned with twisted pines and trailing vines. Every pillar from the nearest to the most distant is sharp and distinct, the gaps between them revealing layer after layer of receding columns fading only in atmospheric haze, not in focus. Wisps of cloud thread between the pillars at mid-height, and the canopy below is a dense, unbroken green. The light is soft and overcast, eliminating harsh shadows and rendering the stone in uniform tones of grey, ochre, and rust.
